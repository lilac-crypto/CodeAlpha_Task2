Understanding Phishing Attacks

1. Phishing Emails

Phishing emails often try to trick you into giving away sensitive information or downloading malware. Watch out for:

  • Poor spelling and grammar mistakes.
  • Urgent or threatening language to rush you.
  • Suspicious sender addresses or unknown contacts.
  • Links that don’t match the displayed URL or redirect to odd sites.
  • Unexpected attachments or requests for personal info.

2. Phishing Websites

Fake websites can look identical to real ones. To protect yourself:

  • Always check the URL carefully for typos or strange domains.
  • Look for HTTPS and the lock icon in the address bar.
  • Be cautious about entering passwords, credit card numbers, or other personal data.
  • If unsure, go to the website directly instead of clicking links.
Tip: Bookmark trusted websites and use those bookmarks to avoid phishing redirects.

3. Social Engineering

Attackers may try to manipulate you into revealing info or taking action. Be aware of:

  • Unexpected calls or messages asking for sensitive info.
  • Pressure tactics like pretending to be urgent or authoritative.
  • Requests for passwords, verification codes, or personal data.
  • Fake tech support or “helpful” contacts that try to gain trust.
Tip: Always verify identity independently before sharing information or granting access.
